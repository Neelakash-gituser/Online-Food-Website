"If food is an art , then Shorshe is the Picasso" - Sanjeev Kapoor

The story of Shorshe began out of a garage space at Ekdalia, Kolkata in March 2003. Two friends from diverse backgrounds with no formal F & B training but having a passion for food and people, reckoned from their own personal experiences that there was a vacuum in Kolkata for simple 'home style' Bengali food. The name Kheye-Jao was chosen from a popular Bengali film song rendered by the legendary singer Hemanta Mukherjee. Within a month of opening the first outlet, Kheye-Jao struck a chord with the masses. A combination of filling up the vacuum in the market along with the food, the brand name, the affordability, the simple environment and a bit of media information...all these played a role in attracting the crowds. Thereafter the story changed completely...by May 2003 we had huge crowds waiting outside our little outlet on the weekends...it was embarrassing and a time had come to think ahead. In the course of the next few years the market driven demand resulted in Kheye-Jao's expansion in Kolkata. The team evolved into a structured format in marketing, operations and CRM areas. The customers...foodies from all over India and many parts of the world including celebrities from different walks of life...contributed immensely in establishing Kheye-Jao. The media also played a major role in promoting brand Kheye-Jao. Shorshe expanded beyond Kolkata to Bengaluru, Siliguri and Puri between 2009 and 2015 and the expansion plans are ongoing .
